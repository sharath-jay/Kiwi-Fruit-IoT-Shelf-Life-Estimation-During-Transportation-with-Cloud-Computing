# Kiwi-Fruit-IoT-Shelf-Life-Estimation-During-Transportation-with-Cloud-Computing

With the increased attention on healthy eating among consumers, it has become critical for retailers to maintain perishable food products such as vegetables and fruits at high quality. Days of shelf life, which is a proxy for food quality depends on humidity, temperature, and gases (ammonium) produced as the produce decays. An IoT system is developed to monitor the quality of kiwi fruit using three measurement variables: 
1) MKRH: mean kinetic relative humidity
2) MKT: mean kinetic temperature
3) NH4: mean ammonium

“Black spots”, the signs of rotting, are also recorded (BlackSpot = 0 means the fruit has no black spots and BlackSpot = 1 means the fruit black spot at the time of measurement). The variable of interest for a retailer would be the shelf life of the product given in Days, to inform inventory replenishment decisions as well as on-the-shelf discounts of produce that is still good to be consumed but not appropriate for more than a few days’ storage.
